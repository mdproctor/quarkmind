Plugin levels — from fastest to highest-level
Level 1 — Frame Observer
Hook: registered listener on GameObserver · runs every SC2 frame
~22fps
Sees raw GameState before it reaches CaseFile. Can write directly to IntentQueue for frame-accurate responses.
Suited for: raw ML inference on frame data, emergency interrupt handlers, frame-accurate unit micromanagement
Level 2 — CaseFile Reactor
Hook: CaseFile.onChange() listener · fires on any CaseFile write
reactive
Observes the full blackboard. Can react to changes made by other TaskDefinitions, not just SC2 observations. Can write back to CaseFile or IntentQueue.
Suited for: Drools watching the whole CaseFile, discrepancy detectors, cross-concern coordination rules
Level 3 — Planning Strategy
Hook: CaseHub PlanningStrategy · runs between CaseEngine cycles
control plane
Directs what the CaseEngine does next — which TaskDefinitions are prioritised, suspended, or cancelled. Sees all PlanItems and CasePlanModel state.
Suited for: high-level strategy selection, crisis response (incoming attack → suspend economics → elevate tactics), CMMN case plan manipulation
Level 4 — TaskDefinition / Worker
Hook: CaseHub TaskDefinition (called) or Worker (autonomous) · data-driven activation
data plane
Called when entry criteria are met (reads specific CaseFile keys). Writes results back to CaseFile or intents to IntentQueue. Worker variant runs autonomously on its own thread.
Suited for: StrategyTask, EconomicsTask, TacticsTask, ScoutingTask — and their Drools/Flow/CaseHub replacements
Shared state access: Any plugin at any level can read/write CaseFile and write to IntentQueue. These are not controlled by a single layer — they are the common substrate all plugins share. Conflict resolution for IntentQueue is handled by PlanningStrategy (Level 3) or by typed intent ownership per unit.
